# Findwell - Therapist Survey

## Purpose

This document captures the therapist survey developed by the Findwell founding team, including the thinking and editorial rationale behind the final question set. The survey is designed to be sent to professional therapists via the Suncoast Mental Health Counseling Association (SMHCA) and Dr. Camp's professional network.

---

## Survey Goals

- Deepen understanding of the challenges therapists face when finding and connecting with high-quality, committed patients
- Gather data to inform product design, specifically the therapist-side matching profile and algorithm inputs
- Identify therapists interested in participating in future focus groups or conversations
- Validate the core market problem from the therapist's perspective

**Important framing distinction:** This is a pain point discovery survey, not a practice development survey or a directory profile exercise. Questions are designed to surface friction and frustration, not to catalog therapist credentials.

---

## Delivery Approach

- **Platform:** Typeform or Google Forms (Typeform preferred, since it feels less clinical and more consistent with the Findwell brand)
- **Anonymity:** Responses are fully anonymous by default
- **Opt-in:** An optional contact opt-in appears at the end of the survey only, after respondents have answered candidly, for those interested in joining future focus groups or conversations
- **Length target:** 10 minutes or less
- **Outreach:** Sent via Dr. Camp and Valentino (SMHCA) to professional therapist networks and member databases

---

## Editorial Principles

The original question set was reviewed and revised against the following criteria:

1. Does the question deepen understanding of the therapist pain point, or does it drift toward validating our solution?
2. Is the question neutral enough to avoid priming the respondent toward a particular answer?
3. Is it appropriately scoped, neither so broad it yields unusable data nor so narrow it misses nuance?
4. Does the overall set tell a coherent story we can use to refine the product and strengthen the pitch?

Questions better suited to a live focus group conversation were removed from the survey to keep it focused and respectful of respondents' time.

---

## Questions Removed and Why

- **"What types of marketing or advertising platforms do you use?"** Competitive intelligence but not a pain point question. Tells us where they advertise, not why finding patients is hard.
- **"Do you have values or life experiences you feel comfortable sharing with prospective clients?"** Too personal and nuanced for a written survey. Better explored in a focus group conversation.
- **"Have you received feedback from a client that gave you valuable insight on your approach?"** Interesting therapeutically but doesn't serve product discovery. Yields inconsistent, hard-to-act-on responses.
- **"What type of peer networking do you participate in?"** Lower priority at this stage. Better suited for a follow-up conversation.
- **"What has your experience with insurance panels been like?"** Opens a complex conversation that could derail survey focus. The deeper nuance is better explored in focus groups.

**Q3. What referral sources have you tried with little success?**
*(Open text)*

*Rationale: One of the strongest questions in the set. Therapists venting about what doesn't work surfaces pain point data directly, and is likely to name Psychology Today and BetterHelp unprompted, validating the core narrative without leading the witness.*

---

**Q4. Do you maintain a professional social media presence?**
- Yes
- No

*If yes: Which platforms do you use?*
*(Short open text)*

*Rationale: Helps understand how therapists currently market themselves and whether they are tech-forward. Relevant to go-to-market strategy.*

---

**Q5. Are there specific presenting concerns, age groups, or treatment modalities you prefer not to work with?**
*(Open text)*

*We ask this to better understand how therapists define a good fit, not to make any judgment about your practice.*

*Rationale: Elevated and expanded from the original. Directly surfaces the matching criteria from the therapist side: modalities they won't use, populations they won't serve. This data feeds directly into algorithm design.*

---

**Q6. How do you currently determine whether a prospective client is a good fit before your first session?**
*(Open text)*

*Rationale: New question. Directly surfaces the screening pain point that Findwell solves. Lets therapists describe the problem in their own words.*

---

**Q7. How much time on average do you spend on intake and screening activities before taking on a new client?**
- Less than 30 minutes
- 30 to 60 minutes
- 1 to 2 hours
- More than 2 hours

*Rationale: New question. Quantifies the burden of the current screening process, important data for the investor pitch and product positioning.*

---

**Q8. Of the prospective clients you meet with for an initial consultation, roughly what percentage do you continue working with?**
- Less than 25%
- 25 to 50%
- 50 to 75%
- More than 75%

*What are the most common reasons a prospective client isn't a good fit?*
*(Open text)*

*Rationale: Reframed from original "conversion rate" language which felt too sales-oriented for a therapist audience. The follow-up question is where the real product insight lives.*

---

**Q9. How many new clients would you ideally want to add to your practice?**
- 1 to 2
- 3 to 5
- 6 to 10
- More than 10

*How close are you currently to your ideal caseload?*
- I'm at capacity
- I have some room
- I have significant room
- I'm just starting to build my practice

*Rationale: Simple and quantitative. Surfaces the gap between where therapists are and where they want to be, the core demand signal for Findwell's therapist value proposition.*
